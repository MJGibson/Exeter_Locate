Exeter Locate

A citizen science Data collection project – Information

 

The Exeter Location Application is designed to facilitate the average citizen to participate in assisting a scientific project to collect anonymous tracking information and data on the local radio environment (for example, Wi-Fi and bluetooth). This data will then be used for further research into future tracking technologies which could offer users a far less battery intensive option over the usual Global Position System (GPS). The page explains how and why Exeter Locate collects this data.

What data is collected

 

Tracking information is collected at semi regular intervals, that is at a regular interval but with some pseudorandom variation. GPS is the primary form of location data collected, but also some accelerometer and magnetometer sensor data is also collected. GPS will detail you location on the earth surface with a given accuracy level. The accelerometer detects the orientation and movement of your device, whereas the magnetometer works like a 3D compass but is also affected by local magnetic field.

In terms of the local radio environment data is collected about the possible Wi-Fi access points which are available to each area, and Bluetooth Low-Energy (BLE) beacons. The data includes the unique identifier of the Wi-fi access-point or BLE beacon, known as the Media Access Control (MAC) Address, as well as the Received Signal Strength Indicator (RSSI).

Finally, the application will generate a random unique identifier which is attached to the above data and guarantees anonymity. All collected data is then posted to our servers via a secure encrypted internet connection.

 

How the data is collected

 

Data collection is limited by a Geo-Fence, that is once the regular location check detects the devices’ location is not on the University Of Exeter (UoE) Stretham campus (as defined by the area in Figure 1, below) then data collection, apart from further location checks are halted. Location data continues to be checked by the application in order to see if the device determine if the device re-enters the geofence, however no data is sent the server until the device re-enters the geofenced area.

Figure 1 - Streatham Campus area

Due to the way the application stores and sends the Unique Random Identifier with the collected data, it will also be possible to track individual route and paths while inside the geofenced area, although this data remains anonymised. It may be possible to deduce an individual’s likely identify from their location data, but it is impossible to confirm this through the data its self.

 

Length of time we keep your data

 

This data maybe held indefinitely, and form part of example data set utilised for future research.

 

Keep you information secure

 

The privacy of volunteers in this project is taken very seriously and every effort is taken to ensure that privacy is maintained. The unique random identifier used to identify individuals’ devices data is generated by a pseudo random routine on the volunteer’s device, and therefore can not be associated with any particular individual without the volunteers providing this additional detail. The data is sent from the device to our servers via a secure encrypted internet connection.

 

 

Your Information rights

 

If you would like to stop contributing data to the project at any time, the application is controlled through the start/stop button and should clearly display if data is currently being collected.

In order to realise some of the benefits of collecting this data, it is important to maintain the same unique random identifier within each application. It would therefore technically be possible to deduce from the location data whom and individual is, but this is never the intended use. This cannot be confirm through the data unless the unique random identifier is tied to an individual unless the individual provides the unique random identifier to use. No records or individuals are maintained other than the unique random identifier.

The rights under articles 15 to 20 of the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) do not apply if the University of Exeter is not in a position to identify a specific individual, unless the individual provides additional information to enable identification. Only providing the unique random identifier does not establish a definite link between that individual and the anonymised location and radio data.

This also means we are unable to provide data in response to any request to access the location and radio environment data generated by your device.

Should you have a query about how your data is handle please contact the RIBA2Reality project team at riba2reality@exeter.ac.uk
